# 9. Buckling

Axial loads on a beam that lay at a distance $d$ from the neutral axis induce a bending moment of:

$$M = d \times P_x$$

The Euler buckling formula gives the critical axial load $P_\text{cr}$ at which a slender, perfectly straight column will buckle under compressive loading:

$$P_\text{cr} = \frac{\pi^2 E I}{(K L)^2}  $$

where:

- $E$ = Young’s modulus of the column material
    
- $I$ = minimum area moment of inertia of the column cross-section
    
- $L$ = actual length of the column
    
- $K$ = column effective length factor, which depends on end conditions:
    
    - $K = 1$ for pinned-pinned
        
    - $K = 0.5$ for fixed-fixed
        
    - $K = 2$ for fixed-free (cantilever)
        
    - $K \approx 0.7$ for fixed-pinned
        

This formula assumes the column is perfectly straight, elastic, and slender, with axial loading only.
