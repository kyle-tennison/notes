# R13. Radiation Exchange Between Surfaces

> This class limits the discussion to opaque diffuse gray surfaces.


The **view factor** $F_{ij}$ is "the fraction of the radiation leaving surface $i$ that is intercepted by surface $j$."
The subscripts give the direction, so $i\to j \Rightarrow F_{ij}$  and $j \to i \Rightarrow F_{ji}$. It's important to realize that $F_{ij}\ne F_{ji}$ except when their areas are equal.

![](https://docs.cemosis.fr/ibat/latest/models/_images/rht/view-factor-geometry.png)

The view factor for $i\to j$ is:

$$F_{ij} = \frac{1}{A_i} \int_{A_i}\int_{A_j}\frac{\cos \theta_i\cos\theta_j}{\pi R^2}dA_idA_j$$

And for $j\to i$:

$$F_{ji} = \frac{1}{A_j}\int_{A_j}\int_{A_i}\frac{\cos \theta_j\cos\theta_i}{\pi R^2}dA_j\,dA_i$$

It follows that:

$$A_iF_{ij} = A_jF_{ji}$$

Is known as the **reciprocity relation**. 

For surfaces in an enclosure made up of $N$ surfaces, the *summation rule* (also known as the *enclosure law*) states:

$$\sum_{j=1}^NF_{ij}=1$$
And, for concave surfaces, it is possible for a surface to "see itself" and $F_{ii}\ne 0$. 

There are $N^2$ view factors for $N$ surfaces in an enclosure; this can be seen when written in matrix form:

$$
\begin{bmatrix} F_{11} & F_{12} & \cdots & F_{1N} \\ F_{21} & F_{22} & \cdots & F_{2N} \\ \vdots & & \ddots & \vdots \\ F_{N1} & F_{N2} & \cdots & F_{NN} \end{bmatrix}
$$

Each *row* can be used with the summation rule. But you rarely need to find all of these to solve a problem.

## Blackbody Radiation Exchange

Radiation exchange between two black bodies is relatively easy because they do not reflect; the only transfer is via radiation/absorption. 

The radiation leaving surface $i$ and is being *intercepted* by surface $j$ is:

$$q_{i \to j} = (A_iJ_i)F_{ij} = A_iF_{ij}E_{bi}$$

where $J_i$ is the [radiosity](R12.%20Radiation.md#^radiosity), which is just $E_{bi}$ for the black surface.

The same can be expressed for ${j\to i}$:

$$q_{j \to i} = A_jF_{ji}E_{bj}$$

The net radiative exchange is simply the difference between these two:

$$\boxed{q_{ij} = q_{i \to j} - q_{j\to i} = A_iF_{ij}\sigma(T_i^4-T_j^4)}$$
If the surface is in an enclosure of $N$ black surfaces, then:

$$q_i = \sum^N_{j=1}A_iF_{ij}\sigma(T_i^4-T_j^4)$$

where $q_i$ is the net transfer of radiation from surface $i$.
