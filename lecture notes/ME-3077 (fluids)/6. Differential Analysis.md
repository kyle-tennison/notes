### 6.1.2 Vorticity

Vorticity is defined as:

$$\boldsymbol \zeta =  \nabla \times \mathbf V$$

It's just the curl of the velocity field. You evaluate this with:

$$\nabla \times \mathbf{V} =
\begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
\frac{\partial}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \\
v_x & v_y & v_z
\end{vmatrix}
$$

The angular velocity vector is simply:

$$\boldsymbol\omega = \frac 12 \boldsymbol\zeta$$

If the vorticity is zero, the fluid is **irrotational**.

## 6.2.3 Stream Function

The stream function in 2D is:

$$u = \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial y}, \quad v = -\frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial x}$$

The flow rate per unit depth $q$ across any line connecting point $A$ to $B$ is:

$$q = \Psi_B - \Psi_A$$

## 6.8.1

For *Newtonian* fluids, the relationship between stress and deformation is:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\sigma_{xx} &= -p + 2 \mu \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} \\ 
\sigma_{yy} &= -p + 2 \mu \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \\ 
\sigma_{zz} &= -p + 2 \mu \frac{\partial w}{\partial z} \\ 
\sigma_{xy} &= \mu \left( \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} \right) \\ 
\sigma_{xz} &= \mu \left( \frac{\partial u}{\partial z} + \frac{\partial w}{\partial x} \right) \\ 
\sigma_{yz} &= \mu \left( \frac{\partial v}{\partial z} + \frac{\partial w}{\partial y} \right)
\end{aligned}

$$
