# L3. Spring Systems

## Transfer Functions

A forced system might look something like this:

$$m\ddot x + b \dot x +kx = u$$

where $u$ is some *forcing function*. The **transfer function** is defined as:

$$G = \frac{X(s)}{U(s)} = \frac{\mathscr L(x(t))}{\mathscr L(u(t))}$$

such that we can get a relationship between input/output.


## Natural Frequency


When solving a system, a 2nd order linear ODE with the form of:

$$A\ddot x + B x = 0$$

will have the natural frequency of:

$$\omega_0 = \sqrt{\frac{B}{A}}$$

## Damper Dissipated Energy 

A viscous damping component has $F=-b\dot x$. The energy dissipated from this is:

$$\int_0^x |F|\,dx = \int_0^x b \dot x \,dx= b \int_0^T\dot x \frac{dx}{dt}dt = b \int_0^T \dot x ^2 \,dt$$

Regardless of the sign, the energy is always dissipated (leaving sys).


## Energy Method of Modeling

You can derive equations of motion *when energy is conserved* (no dampers) using energy equations.

$$T+U = \text{const}$$

where $T$ is kinetic energy and $U$ is potential energy. In a simple spring-mass system, this might look like:

$$\frac 12  m\dot x^2 + \frac 12 kx^2 = \text{const}$$

Taking the derivative gives:

$$\frac{d}{dt}\left(T+U\right)= m \dot x \ddot x + k x \dot x = \dot x (m\ddot x + kx) =0$$

Because we know $\dot x \ne 0 \forall t$, the factor $m \ddot x + kx$ must $=0$.
